# OnePlus Mic Test — Audio Quality Analysis

## Key Findings

**Best address pattern by SNR:** Top Mic (25.1 dB)
**Best address pattern by RMS:** Phone-to-Ear (-23.3 dBFS)
**Optimal proximity distance:** 0.5 cm (SNR 23.7 dB)

## Actionable Recommendations

1. **Address the center of the screen, not the bottom mic port.** The bottom mic (near USB-C) — where most people instinctively aim — had the worst SNR (17.1 dB). Center/LCD address gives better balanced, warmer-sounding audio (SNR 21.0 dB, centroid 587 Hz vs 673 Hz). Top mic had the highest raw SNR (25.1 dB) but a brighter/thinner tonal quality (centroid 792 Hz).
2. **Keep 10–15cm distance from the screen.** This range gives the best balance of signal strength and clarity (SNR 22.7 dB). At 3cm, RMS is highest (-22.1 dBFS) but SNR drops (19.3 dB) due to proximity bass boost and distortion. At 30cm, both RMS and SNR fall off (-27.2 dBFS, 18.4 dB). The 0.5cm "touching" test had high SNR (23.7 dB) but is impractical and risks clipping.
3. **Use a phone case freely.** No measurable degradation — in fact the case showed slightly *higher* SNR (27.8 vs 21.0 dB) and a small centroid shift upward (772 vs 628 Hz), likely due to case resonance. With mic port cutouts, there is no reason to remove the case for recording.
4. **Use a stand or mount to eliminate handling noise.** Handling noise (segment 14) showed the highest dynamic range spikes in the entire test. Even a basic stand (Ulanzi selfie stick) showed good vibration rejection with only minor transfer when shaken hard.
5. **Stay on-axis.** Off-axis positions (45° or more) drop signal by ~4 dB (from -25.6 to -30.0 dBFS) and shift spectral content. During the 360° walk, quality degraded most at 180° (behind the phone).
6. **For the best practical setup:** Phone on a stand, case on, 10–15cm from screen, speaking at the LCD — not the mic port.
